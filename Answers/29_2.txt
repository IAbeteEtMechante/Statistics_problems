as shown on the picture, for normal distribution, 
equating the first and second moments gives us:

mu^    = sample mean
sigma^ = s

in the particular case of this exercise a simple R code can give us:

mu^    = 4.71
sigma^ = 1.54

################R code################
> aa <- c(4.93, 6.82, 3.12, 7.57, 3.04, 4.98, 4.62, 4.84, 2.95, 4.22)
> mean(aa)
[1] 4.709
> sd(aa)
[1] 1.543182
